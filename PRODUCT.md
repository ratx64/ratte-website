# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

RatteCS serves a mixed audience: existing stream viewers looking for official links, new CS2 players searching for settings or gear, affiliate and coupon visitors checking partner codes, and collaborators who need a clear contact path. They usually arrive from social profiles, streams, search, embeds, or shared links, and they need to quickly confirm they are in the right place, pick a destination, and trust that partner links are transparent.

## Product Purpose

The site is the official RatteCS link hub. It centralizes social profiles, live platforms, CS2 settings, Steam links, partner offers, discount codes, and business contact details in one fast, searchable page. Success means visitors can find the right link without friction, partner codes are visible without feeling pushy, and the page strengthens Ratte's creator identity instead of feeling like a generic link-tree clone.

## Brand Personality

Moody, compact, creator-native. The brand should feel like the current site, but more refined: dark, focused, gaming-adjacent, lightly atmospheric, and direct. It should keep the personal creator feel, the anime profile image, the red and blue ambience, and the tight link-card structure while improving hierarchy, clarity, polish, and trust signals.

## Anti-references

Avoid a generic Linktree clone, loud esports neon clutter, over-polished SaaS marketing, crypto-style glow overload, and anything that turns the page into a broad landing page instead of a focused creator hub. Avoid redesigns that erase the current vibe, make the page too bright, bury partner disclosures, or make affiliate links feel deceptive or spammy.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the current vibe, raise the craft.
2. Make the next click obvious.
3. Treat affiliate trust as part of the brand.
4. Stay compact, but not cramped.
5. Use atmosphere as support, never as noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, descriptive image text, and reduced-motion support. Dark mode contrast matters because the primary experience is a dark, moody interface; partner badges, low-contrast secondary copy, and tiny metadata should remain readable on mobile and desktop.
